LED Light-boxes

My youngest son is on the Autism spectrum and has exhibited several unique behaviors throughout the last 9 years. When he was just 3 years of age he liked to turn light switches on and off repeatedly. After having several bulbs burnout, I decided to make him a box. I took a black plastic box and drilled holes to create places for switches, LEDs, toggles, and buttons. I spent several hours soldering connections and wiring until it was completed. My son loved the box so much, he (mostly) stopped his obsessive use of our lights. To this day I have a tackle box full of electronic wiring components making little additions to things around the house and fixing toys that otherwise would thrown out.
